Deployment Model Overview

Choosing between on-premises and cloud-based AI infrastructure is a critical decision that impacts cost, performance, security, scalability, and operational complexity. On-premises deployments provide full control and potentially lower long-term costs for large-scale operations, while cloud solutions offer flexibility, rapid scaling, and reduced upfront capital investment. Many organizations adopt hybrid approaches that combine both models to optimize for different workload requirements.

On-Premises Infrastructure

On-premises AI infrastructure involves deploying and managing GPU servers, networking, and storage within your own data center or colocation facility. This model provides complete control over hardware, software, and data, making it suitable for organizations with specific requirements for data sovereignty, security, or performance.

Advantages of On-Premises

Full Control

Complete control over hardware configuration, software stack, security policies, and operational procedures. No dependency on cloud provider limitations.

Cost Efficiency

Lower long-term costs for sustained, high-utilization workloads. No per-hour charges, and hardware can be fully depreciated over time.

Data Sovereignty

Complete data control and compliance with data residency requirements. Critical for regulated industries and sensitive data.

Performance

Potentially better performance with dedicated hardware, optimized networking (InfiniBand), and no multi-tenancy overhead.

Challenges of On-Premises

  • High Upfront Costs: Significant capital investment required for hardware, data center space, and infrastructure
  • Long Deployment Time: Weeks to months for procurement, installation, and configuration
  • Operational Overhead: Requires skilled IT staff for hardware management, maintenance, and troubleshooting
  • Limited Scalability: Scaling requires purchasing additional hardware, which takes time
  • Maintenance Responsibility: Hardware failures, updates, and maintenance are your responsibility
  • Capacity Planning: Must accurately predict capacity needs to avoid over-provisioning or under-provisioning

On-Premises Use Cases

  • Large-Scale Training: Organizations training large models continuously with predictable workloads
  • Regulated Industries: Healthcare, finance, government with strict data residency requirements
  • High-Performance Requirements: Workloads requiring maximum performance and low latency
  • Cost Optimization: Organizations with sustained high utilization where on-premises is more cost-effective
  • Custom Hardware: Need for specialized hardware configurations not available in cloud

On-Premises Cost Example: A DGX A100 system costs approximately $200K. With 5-year depreciation and operational costs, annual cost is ~$60K. If utilized 80% of the time for training, cost per GPU-hour is approximately $0.85, compared to $3-5 per GPU-hour in the cloud. For sustained workloads, on-premises can be 3-5x more cost-effective.

Cloud-Based Infrastructure

Cloud-based AI infrastructure provides access to GPU resources through cloud providers like AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, or NVIDIA NGC. This model offers pay-as-you-go pricing, elastic scaling, and managed services, making it ideal for organizations that need flexibility and rapid deployment.

Advantages of Cloud-Based

Elastic Scaling

Scale up or down instantly based on workload demands. No need to purchase hardware upfront or wait for deployment.

Low Upfront Cost

Pay only for what you use. No large capital investment required. Convert capital expenses to operational expenses.

Managed Services

Cloud providers handle hardware maintenance, updates, and infrastructure management, reducing operational overhead.

Global Reach

Deploy workloads in multiple regions for low-latency access and disaster recovery. Access to latest hardware without purchasing.

Challenges of Cloud-Based

  • Higher Long-Term Costs: For sustained workloads, cloud costs can exceed on-premises over time
  • Vendor Lock-in: Dependence on cloud provider's services, APIs, and pricing models
  • Data Egress Costs: Costs for transferring large datasets out of the cloud can be significant
  • Limited Control: Less control over hardware configuration, software versions, and security policies
  • Multi-Tenancy Overhead: Shared infrastructure may have performance variability
  • Compliance Concerns: Data residency and compliance requirements may limit cloud options

Cloud Use Cases

  • Variable Workloads: Workloads with unpredictable or variable demand patterns
  • Rapid Prototyping: Quick experimentation and development without hardware procurement delays
  • Burst Capacity: Handling peak loads that exceed on-premises capacity
  • Startups: Organizations without capital for upfront hardware investment
  • Geographic Distribution: Need for global deployment and low-latency access
  • Managed Services: Organizations preferring managed infrastructure over self-management
Cloud Cost Example: AWS p4d.24xlarge instance with 8x A100 GPUs costs approximately $32/hour. For a workload running 24/7 for a year, annual cost is ~$280K. For the same hardware on-premises (DGX A100), with 5-year depreciation and operational costs, annual cost is ~$60K. Cloud is more expensive for sustained workloads but offers flexibility.

Hybrid Approach

Hybrid deployments combine on-premises and cloud infrastructure to optimize for different workload requirements. This approach allows organizations to leverage the benefits of both models while mitigating their respective limitations.

Hybrid Architecture Patterns

  • Base Capacity On-Premises: Run steady-state workloads on-premises, burst to cloud for peaks
  • Development in Cloud: Develop and test in cloud, deploy production on-premises
  • Data On-Premises, Compute in Cloud: Keep sensitive data on-premises, use cloud for compute
  • Multi-Cloud: Use multiple cloud providers to avoid vendor lock-in and optimize costs
  • Cloudbursting: Automatically scale to cloud when on-premises capacity is exceeded

Hybrid Benefits

  • Cost Optimization: Use on-premises for cost-effective base capacity, cloud for variable demand
  • Flexibility: Adapt to changing workload requirements without full commitment to one model
  • Risk Mitigation: Reduce dependency on single infrastructure model
  • Workload Optimization: Match workload characteristics to appropriate infrastructure
  • Gradual Migration: Transition gradually from one model to another

Hybrid Challenges

  • Complexity: Managing two different infrastructure environments increases operational complexity
  • Data Movement: Transferring data between on-premises and cloud can be slow and costly
  • Consistency: Maintaining consistent software stacks and configurations across environments
  • Network Connectivity: Requires reliable, high-bandwidth connectivity between environments
  • Management Overhead: Need for tools and processes to manage hybrid infrastructure

Decision Framework

Use a structured decision framework to evaluate deployment options based on your specific requirements, constraints, and objectives.

Decision Criteria

  • Workload Characteristics: Steady-state vs variable, predictable vs unpredictable demand
  • Scale: Small-scale experimentation vs large-scale production
  • Budget: Capital budget availability vs operational budget preference
  • Time to Market: Urgency of deployment and time constraints
  • Compliance Requirements: Data residency, regulatory requirements, security policies
  • Technical Expertise: In-house capabilities for infrastructure management
  • Performance Requirements: Latency, throughput, and consistency needs
  • Long-Term Strategy: Growth plans and future scalability needs

Recommendations by Scenario

Choose On-Premises If:

  • Sustained high utilization (>70%)
  • Strict data residency requirements
  • Large-scale operations (100+ GPUs)
  • Custom hardware needs
  • Capital budget available

Choose Cloud If:

  • Variable or unpredictable workloads
  • Rapid prototyping needs
  • Limited capital budget
  • Need for global deployment
  • Prefer managed services

Choose Hybrid If:

  • Mix of steady-state and variable workloads
  • Want to optimize costs per workload
  • Gradual migration strategy
  • Need flexibility and risk mitigation
  • Have both capital and operational budget
Decision Process: 1. Assess workload characteristics (utilization, variability, scale)
2. Evaluate budget constraints (capital vs operational)
3. Review compliance and security requirements
4. Assess technical capabilities and operational preferences
5. Calculate 3-year TCO for each option
6. Score each option against decision criteria
7. Consider hybrid approach if benefits outweigh complexity
8. Start with pilot deployment to validate decision
